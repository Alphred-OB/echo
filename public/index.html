Sign in with sound.
No passwords. Ever.

Your laptop plays an inaudible one-time code. Your phone hears it, asks for your approval, and signs you in. Nothing to type, nothing to phish, nothing to leak.

How it works

Inaudible handshake The sign-in page plays a single-use cryptographic code at 18–20 kHz — above human hearing, well within your phone's.
Your phone is the key A private key generated on your phone — and unable to leave it — signs the code. You approve every sign-in.
Replay-proof by design Every sign-in plays a different sound. Each code is random, dies on first use, and expires in 30 seconds — a recording is worthless.
Proximity is the point Ultrasound carries about two metres. If your phone can hear the laptop, you are standing at it — that is the proof.
