The Physics & Cryptography of Echo

A detailed look into how sound-waves and hardware-backed key pairs combine to form a secure, passwordless authentication protocol.

Acoustic Channel (ggwave)

Audio modulation at 18–20 kHz.

Echo transmits single-use session tokens over high-frequency audio. The payload is modulated using Reed-Solomon error correction and processed via a compiled WebAssembly binary in the browser.

Frequency Selection: 18–20 kHz is selected because it is above the threshold of normal human hearing but remains within the sample range (44.1/48 kHz) of generic mobile microphones.

Device-Bound Cryptography

Asymmetric ECDSA signing.

During account enrollment, the smartphone generates a non-exportable P-256 Elliptic Curve key pair. The private key is stored securely in the browser's IndexedDB and never leaves the device.

Signature payload structure:
"echo-v1|<nonce>|<deviceId>"
Algorithm: ECDSA / SHA-256
Curve Name: NIST P-256

Phishing Mitigation: Because the signature includes the domain-separation prefix (`echo-v1`) and the unique device identifier, a stolen signature cannot be re-used to claim a session on a different domain.
