Core Capabilities

Built for security. Designed for simplicity.

Echo combines three layers of physical and cryptographic verification to eliminate password vulnerabilities.

Cryptographic Keys

Device-bound P-256 ECDSA key pairs generated via WebCrypto API. Private keys are non-exportable and safely isolated in local storage.

Acoustic Channel

Payloads encoded via ggwave WASM and modulated to 18–20 kHz near-ultrasound. Bypasses standard human hearing with built-in error correction.

Proximity Defense

Ultrasound range is naturally constrained to ~2 meters. Prevents remote phishers from tricking users or tapping into authentication channels.

Real-world Security

Challenges & How We Solve Them

How Echo handles physical limitations, background noise, and remote attackers.

Replay Attacks (Recorded Sound)

Challenge: An attacker records the sound played by your computer speaker and plays it back later to try to log in.

✔ Solution: Every login uses a brand-new random code. The server deletes the code instantly when verified, and it expires after 30 seconds. Additionally, requesting a new code instantly invalidates any old code.

Stolen or Unlocked Devices

Challenge: An attacker steals your physical phone to approve a login request.

✔ Solution: We assume personal phones are naturally secured via lock-screen checks (fingerprint or PIN). Because the key is protected by the phone's secure storage, it remains inaccessible without unlocking. You can also instantly revoke any stolen phone from another active browser.

Loud Noise & Hardware Issues

Challenge: Ambient noise (music, talking) or bad quality speakers/microphones block the sound wave.

✔ Solution: The signal uses audio error correction. If the silent ultrasound fails, the app lets you play an audible code, or sign in using a backup password.

Remote Phishing Attempts

Challenge: A fake login website tries to trick your phone into signing an approval request.

✔ Solution: The signature is mathematically tied to the specific domain. The phone communicates directly with the real server, keeping the middleman out of the loop.

Phone Unavailable / Unwanted

Challenge: You do not want to use your phone to sign in, or your phone battery is dead.

✔ Solution: You can choose to sign in using your backup account password, or use one of the single-use recovery codes generated from your dashboard.

Browser Compatibility

Challenge: Your older computer or phone doesn\'t support the secure microphone or cryptography APIs.

✔ Solution: The system detects feature support immediately. If standard APIs are missing, the login falls back automatically to traditional password verification.
